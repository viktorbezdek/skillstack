# Critical Intuition & Sixth Sense

This skill enables sophisticated analytical and intuitive thinking to help users detect subtle patterns, read between the lines, reason rigorously, sense hidden dynamics, and make sound judgments under uncertainty.

## When to Use This Skill

Apply when the user requests:
- "Read between the lines"
- "What am I missing?"
- "Analyze this critically"
- "What's really going on here?"
- "Trust your intuition" or "what does your gut say?"
- "Early warning signs" or "red flags"
- "Hidden patterns" or "subtle signals"
- Detect unstated assumptions or hidden agendas
- Rigorous reasoning or logical analysis
- Risk assessment or opportunity detection
- Synthesis of complex information
- Judgment under uncertainty

## Core Approach

Critical intuition follows this pattern:

1. **Surface Analysis** - What's explicitly stated and obvious
2. **Deep Reading** - What's implied, hidden, or absent
3. **Pattern Detection** - Recognize significant patterns and anomalies
4. **Critical Evaluation** - Rigorously test reasoning and evidence
5. **Intuitive Synthesis** - Integrate multiple signals into gestalt understanding
6. **Judgment Formation** - Reach sound conclusions despite uncertainty

## Analysis Process

### Step 1: Multi-Level Reading

Analyze at multiple depths:

**Surface level:**
- What is explicitly stated?
- What are the obvious facts?
- What's the apparent situation?

**Subtext level:**
- What's implied but not stated?
- What assumptions are embedded?
- What's the emotional undertone?
- What's conspicuously absent?

**Meta level:**
- Why is this being communicated this way?
- What are the incentives and constraints?
- What's the broader context?
- What power dynamics are at play?

For advanced reading techniques, read `references/pattern-recognition.md`.

### Step 2: Signal Detection

Identify meaningful patterns and anomalies:

**Pattern recognition:**
- What patterns emerge across multiple data points?
- What's the signal vs. noise distinction?
- Are there meta-patterns (patterns about patterns)?
- What historical patterns does this match?

**Anomaly detection:**
- What doesn't fit expected patterns?
- What's unusually present or absent?
- Where are the discontinuities?
- What surprises or contradicts expectations?

**Micro-signals:**
- Subtle energy shifts
- Timing variations
- Emphasis patterns
- Language hedging
- Topic avoidance

**Gap analysis:**
- What information is missing?
- What would normally be mentioned but isn't?
- Where are logical discontinuities?
- What perspectives are absent?

For subtle signal detection techniques, read `references/sixth-sense.md`.

### Step 3: Critical Reasoning

Apply rigorous logical analysis:

**Argument structure:**
- Identify premises and conclusions
- Evaluate logical validity
- Assess soundness (valid + true premises)
- Detect hidden assumptions

**Fallacy detection:**
- Formal fallacies (logical errors)
- Informal fallacies (reasoning flaws)
- Statistical fallacies
- Motivated reasoning

**Evidence evaluation:**
- Source credibility assessment
- Data quality analysis
- Sample representativeness
- Effect size vs. statistical significance
- Confounding factors

**Assumption testing:**
- List implicit assumptions
- Challenge each assumption
- Test sensitivity to assumptions
- Identify critical assumptions

For comprehensive critical thinking frameworks, read `references/critical-thinking.md`.

### Step 4: Probabilistic Assessment

Quantify uncertainty appropriately:

**Bayesian thinking:**
- What's the prior probability (base rate)?
- How strong is this evidence?
- What's the updated probability?
- What's residual uncertainty?

**Multiple hypotheses:**
- Generate alternative explanations
- Assess relative likelihood
- Identify discriminating evidence
- Track multiple possibilities

**Confidence calibration:**
- How confident should I be?
- Am I over/under-confident?
- What would change my mind?
- What's my track record?

### Step 5: Intuitive Synthesis

Integrate multiple signals holistically:

**Gestalt perception:**
- Combine disparate cues
- See the whole picture
- Pattern completion
- Emergent understanding

**Cross-domain connections:**
- Analogical reasoning
- Structural similarities
- Principle transfer
- Unexpected relationships

**Tacit knowledge:**
- Pattern matching without explicit rules
- Rapid categorization
- Aesthetic judgment (fit, elegance)
- Gut feeling as compressed expertise

**Emotional intelligence:**
- Read subtext and undertones
- Sense interpersonal dynamics
- Detect political undercurrents
- Feel momentum and trajectory

For Intuitive Synthesis techniques, read `references/pattern-recognition.md` section on Intuitive Synthesis.

### Step 6: Early Warning Detection

Identify problems before they become obvious:

**Leading indicators:**
- Upstream signals
- Stress accumulation
- Resilience degradation
- Variance increases

**Trajectory sensing:**
- Momentum assessment (velocity, acceleration)
- Inflection point detection
- Tipping point proximity
- Phase transition signals

**Red flag clusters:**
- Multiple small concerns
- Coincidence accumulation
- Pattern breaks
- "Too good to be true" feelings

**Hidden risks:**
- Single points of failure
- Dependency chains
- Cascading failure paths
- Black swan exposure

For Early Warning Detection details, read `references/sixth-sense.md`.

### Step 7: Judgment Formation

Synthesize into sound conclusions:

**Information integration:**
- Combine quantitative and qualitative
- Triangulate multiple sources
- Resolve conflicts
- Weight by reliability

**Decision under uncertainty:**
- Expected value thinking
- Scenario analysis
- Robust options
- Risk management

**Metacognitive check:**
- How confident am I?
- What biases might affect me?
- What am I missing?
- How can I validate this?

For comprehensive synthesis techniques, read `references/synthesis-frameworks.md`.

## Advanced Techniques

### Reading Between the Lines

**Linguistic cues:**
- Hedging language ("somewhat", "may", "possibly")
- Absolute language ("always", "never", "must")
- Passive voice (avoiding agency)
- Euphemisms and coded language
- Emphasis patterns

**Structural cues:**
- Information sequencing
- Topic transitions
- Paragraph emphasis
- Omissions and deflections
- Frame choices

**Contextual cues:**
- Who benefits from this narrative?
- What's the unstated agenda?
- What constraints shape communication?
- What can't be said directly?

### Sixth Sense Development

**Trust your gut when:**
- You have deep domain expertise
- Pattern-rich environment
- Immediate feedback history
- Consistent track record

**Scrutinize your gut when:**
- Low expertise domain
- Emotionally charged situation
- Wishful thinking possibility
- Random/chaotic environment

**Strengthen intuition:**
- Make predictions and track accuracy
- Rapid feedback loops
- Diverse example exposure
- Reflective practice
- Edge case exploration

### Critical Questioning

**Socratic probing:**
- Clarification: "What exactly do you mean by...?"
- Assumption: "What are you assuming here?"
- Evidence: "What supports this claim?"
- Implication: "What follows from this?"
- Alternative: "How else could we interpret this?"

**Five Whys:**
1. Surface symptom
2. Proximate cause
3. Systemic factor
4. Structural cause
5. Fundamental driver

### Bias Mitigation

**Common biases:**
- Confirmation bias
- Availability bias
- Anchoring
- Dunning-Kruger
- Planning fallacy
- Survivorship bias

**Debiasing techniques:**
- Pre-mortem analysis
- Devil's advocate
- Outside view
- Steelman opposition
- Blind evaluation
- Base rate anchoring

## Synthesis Principles

### Information Hierarchy
1. **Data** - Raw facts and observations
2. **Information** - Organized and contextualized data
3. **Knowledge** - Understood patterns and relationships
4. **Insight** - Non-obvious implications and connections
5. **Wisdom** - Sound judgment and appropriate action

### Integration Methods

**Triangulation:**
- Multiple independent sources
- Different methodologies
- Quantitative + qualitative
- Convergent evidence
- Divergent evidence reconciliation

**Bayesian updating:**
- Start with prior probability
- Update on new evidence
- Weight by evidence strength
- Track multiple hypotheses
- Express residual uncertainty

**Scenario analysis:**
- Best case / worst case / likely case
- High-impact low-probability scenarios
- Early indicators for each
- Decision triggers
- Contingency plans

## Red Flags & Green Flags

### Red Flags (Warning Signs)

**Communication red flags:**
- Excessive hedging or vagueness
- Deflection of direct questions
- Inconsistencies in narrative
- Missing expected details
- "Too good to be true" claims

**Behavioral red flags:**
- Consistency breaks
- Urgency without clear reason
- Bypassed standard procedures
- Missing usual stakeholders
- Resistance to scrutiny

**Analytical red flags:**
- Extraordinary claims without extraordinary evidence
- Cherry-picked data
- Correlation claimed as causation
- Base rate neglect
- Selection bias

### Green Flags (Positive Signals)

**Communication green flags:**
- Transparency and detail
- Acknowledge limitations
- Welcome questions
- Consistent narrative
- Appropriate confidence

**Behavioral green flags:**
- Track record of reliability
- Follows through on commitments
- Admits mistakes
- Seeks feedback
- Demonstrates expertise

**Analytical green flags:**
- Multiple independent sources
- Convergent evidence
- Explicit uncertainty
- Alternative explanations considered
- Falsifiable predictions
