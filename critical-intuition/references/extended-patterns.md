# Critical Intuition - Extended Patterns & Techniques

Advanced techniques, detailed frameworks, and output format templates extracted from the core skill.

## Advanced Techniques

### Reading Between the Lines

**Linguistic cues:**
- Hedging language ("somewhat", "may", "possibly")
- Absolute language ("always", "never", "must")
- Passive voice (avoiding agency)
- Euphemisms and coded language
- Emphasis patterns

**Structural cues:**
- Information sequencing
- Topic transitions
- Paragraph emphasis
- Omissions and deflections
- Frame choices

**Contextual cues:**
- Who benefits from this narrative?
- What's the unstated agenda?
- What constraints shape communication?
- What can't be said directly?

### Sixth Sense Development

**Trust your gut when:**
- You have deep domain expertise
- Pattern-rich environment
- Immediate feedback history
- Consistent track record

**Scrutinize your gut when:**
- Low expertise domain
- Emotionally charged situation
- Wishful thinking possibility
- Random/chaotic environment

**Strengthen intuition:**
- Make predictions and track accuracy
- Rapid feedback loops
- Diverse example exposure
- Reflective practice
- Edge case exploration

### Critical Questioning

**Socratic probing:**
- Clarification: "What exactly do you mean by...?"
- Assumption: "What are you assuming here?"
- Evidence: "What supports this claim?"
- Implication: "What follows from this?"
- Alternative: "How else could we interpret this?"

**Five Whys:**
1. Surface symptom
2. Proximate cause
3. Systemic factor
4. Structural cause
5. Fundamental driver

### Bias Mitigation

**Common biases:**
- Confirmation bias
- Availability bias
- Anchoring
- Dunning-Kruger
- Planning fallacy
- Survivorship bias

**Debiasing techniques:**
- Pre-mortem analysis
- Devil's advocate
- Outside view
- Steelman opposition
- Blind evaluation
- Base rate anchoring

## Synthesis Principles

### Information Hierarchy
1. **Data** - Raw facts and observations
2. **Information** - Organized and contextualized data
3. **Knowledge** - Understood patterns and relationships
4. **Insight** - Non-obvious implications and connections
5. **Wisdom** - Sound judgment and appropriate action

### Integration Methods

**Triangulation:**
- Multiple independent sources
- Different methodologies
- Quantitative + qualitative
- Convergent evidence
- Divergent evidence reconciliation

**Bayesian updating:**
- Start with prior probability
- Update on new evidence
- Weight by evidence strength
- Track multiple hypotheses
- Express residual uncertainty

**Scenario analysis:**
- Best case / worst case / likely case
- High-impact low-probability scenarios
- Early indicators for each
- Decision triggers
- Contingency plans

## Red Flags & Green Flags

### Red Flags (Warning Signs)

**Communication red flags:**
- Excessive hedging or vagueness
- Deflection of direct questions
- Inconsistencies in narrative
- Missing expected details
- "Too good to be true" claims

**Behavioral red flags:**
- Consistency breaks
- Urgency without clear reason
- Bypassed standard procedures
- Missing usual stakeholders
- Resistance to scrutiny

**Analytical red flags:**
- Extraordinary claims without extraordinary evidence
- Cherry-picked data
- Correlation claimed as causation
- Base rate neglect
- Selection bias

### Green Flags (Positive Signals)

**Communication green flags:**
- Transparency and detail
- Acknowledge limitations
- Welcome questions
- Consistent narrative
- Appropriate confidence

**Behavioral green flags:**
- Track record of reliability
- Follows through on commitments
- Admits mistakes
- Seeks feedback
- Demonstrates expertise

**Analytical green flags:**
- Multiple independent sources
- Convergent evidence
- Explicit uncertainty
- Alternative explanations considered
- Falsifiable predictions
